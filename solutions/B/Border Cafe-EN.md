## 6. Customer

- TALK TO customer
  - She speaks in a different language (as a non-native speaker, this was puzzling)
    - "Yth es ow kelwel. Hwi a vynn ow kelwel dhyworth `Kernow` ha ow kelwel gans an gwrys kream gans an jam war an fordh! Yma ow kelwel Kernowek! Rag yth esow dhyworth `Kernow`. Ha yth esow ow kelwel!"
- LOOK AT Cornwall history book
  - Romans to Early Modern: They are likely to be the origin of `Kernow`, the name for Cornwall in den Cornish language
  - Georgians to the Present: (...) preserving the Cornish language and identity
- **NOTE:** Translation from the Cornish:  
  `I am calling. You will call me from Cornwall and call with the clotted cream with the jam on the way. "Cornish is calling! Because you are from Cornwall. And you are calling!`
- USE `Jam first Cream Tea` on customer
